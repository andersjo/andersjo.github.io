Anders Johannsen

I am a part of the Copenhagen Applied Statistical Learning (COASTAL) group at the University of Copenhagen as a two-year post.doc (ending March 2016).

My research focuses on developing robust language technology that allows us to cross time, domain and language barriers. Being able to process and extract meaning from text outside of the mainstream (e.g. historical documents and posts from social media) is instrumental when we wish to analyse culture and language at a larger scale.

Software

  • Hanstholm, a transition-based dependency parser with a completely open feature model. One of the few parsers to support real-valued features.
  • Rungsted, a very fast and easily hackable structured perceptron implementation in Python. Supports cost-sensitive updates, constrained inference, and feature hashing.
  • FrameNet annotation tool. A browser interface for marking up text with frame semantic information. The GUI displays textual descriptions of frame and arguments as needed, alleviating the need to memorize all the definitions.
  • Andreord.dk. Semantic networks presented in a graphical and accessible manner. Available as a public web site here and in a version with cross-language links there.
